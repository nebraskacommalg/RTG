RTG Learning Seminar, Spring 2026: Koszul Algebras

Mondays, 2:30-3:20, in Avery 351

During the Spring 2026 semester, Adam LaClair, with the help of Eloísa Grifo and Mark Walker, will be leading the first RTG Learning Seminar. In this seminar, graduate students, post-docs and faculty will take turns presenting material covering various topics centering on a general theme. It will be similar to the CMinar we ran in the Spring of 2025 (but it will last the entire semester, and will be on a different topic.) The goal is to learn a focused topic in great depth, with the aspiration of getting the participants up to speed on the cutting edge status of a research topic, and perhaps even sparking some new research projects. The topic of this semester's Learning Seminar will be Koszul Algebras. Here is a tentative list of specific topics we plan to discuss:

Resources

  • January 26: In McCullough-Peeva, p.243-4 they introduce the quadratic dual and have some examples, and p.244-5, they use this to define the Priddy resolution and present an example. In Beilinson, Ginzburg, Soergel, p. 483, 2.6 and 2.6.1, they introduce the Priddy complex and prove that it is a resolution when the ring is Koszul.
  • February 2: In Beilinson, Ginzburg, Soergel, Section 2.11, they give a proof of this result. A proof that Koszul implies the stated equality is given in Löfwall, ``Hilbert series, Poincaré series and homotopy Lie algebras of graded algebras — a seminar," Section 1.2. Another proof that the stated equality implies that the ring is Koszul is given in Löfwall, ``On the subalgebra generated by the one dimensional elements in the Yoneda Ext algebra," Theorem 1.2, p.305.
  • February 9: The relevant paper is Fröberg's paper ``Koszul algebras."

Topics:

  • Introduction to Koszul algebras and properties: Priddy resolution, Avramov-Eisenbud-Peeva regularity characterization, rational Poincaré series
  • Proving Koszulness: Quadratic Grobner bases, Froberg's theorem, LG quadratic, Koszul filtrations
  • Examples of Koszul algebras: Quadratic complete intersections, high Veronese algebras, pinched Veroneses (sometimes), quadratic Gorenstein algebras (sometimes), strongly chordal and claw-free binomial edge ideals
  • Other interesting examples: Anick's examples of irrational Poincaré series, Conca's strictness-of-implication examples, Roos's nonlinear syzygies examples, D'Alì-Venturello examples of dependence on characteristic
  • Conjectures: Bounds on Betti numbers, Bogvad's conjecture, Veroneses of non-standard graded polynomial rings
