RTG Learning Seminar, Spring 2026: Koszul Algebras

Time and location Mondays, 2:30-3:20, in Avery 351

During the Spring 2026 semester, Adam LaClair, with the help of Eloísa Grifo and Mark Walker, will be leading the first RTG Learning Seminar. In this seminar, graduate students, post-docs and faculty will take turns presenting material covering various topics centering on a general theme. It will be similar to the CMinar we ran in the Spring of 2025 (but it will last the entire semester, and will be on a different topic.) The goal is to learn a focused topic in great depth, with the aspiration of getting the participants up to speed on the cutting edge status of a research topic, and perhaps even sparking some new research projects. The topic of this semester's Learning Seminar will be Koszul Algebras. Here is a tentative (and incomplete) list of specific topics we plan to discuss:

Topics:

  • Introduction to Koszul algebras and properties: Priddy resolution, Avramov-Eisenbud-Peeva regularity characterization, rational Poincaré series
  • Proving Koszulness: Quadratic Grobner bases, Froberg's theorem, LG quadratic, Koszul filtrations
  • Examples of Koszul algebras: Quadratic complete intersections, high Veronese algebras, pinched Veroneses (sometimes), quadratic Gorenstein algebras (sometimes), strongly chordal and claw-free binomial edge ideals
  • Other interesting examples: Anick's examples of irrational Poincaré series, Conca's strictness-of-implication examples, Roos's nonlinear syzygies examples, D'Alì-Venturello examples of dependence on characteristic
  • Conjectures: Bounds on Betti numbers, Bogvad's conjecture, Veroneses of non-standard graded polynomial rings
